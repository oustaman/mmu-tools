Why This Matters

The Architecture of Information
Is Never Neutral

Findability

Organisation determines whether users reach their goal — or abandon the attempt. Poor IA is invisible friction.

Embedded Assumptions

Every label, grouping, and hierarchy encodes a claim about who the user is. IA is never a neutral container.

Institutional Power

What is foregrounded, what is buried, what has no home — these decisions serve some users and marginalise others.

Your Professional Literacy

Information professionals navigate, maintain, and design these systems. Reading and critiquing IA is a core skill.

The central question: when you look at a navigation system, can you read who it was designed for — and who it was not?

Theoretical Framework

◆ Theory

Information Scent

Pirolli & Card, 1999 — Information Foraging Theory

The Metaphor

Users behave like predators following a scent trail. Labels, snippets, and visual signals predict whether a path leads toward the goal. Users constantly weigh cost against benefit: is following this link worth the effort?

Strong Scent

Label predicts content. User advances with low cognitive cost.

→ "Mortgage Payment Support"

Broken Scent

Ambiguous label. User hesitates, backtracks, or abandons.

→ "Ways to Bank" (for what?)

Key Insight

Scent must match the target user's vocabulary. The same label can be strong for one user and broken for another.

Implication

Different markets, different users → different scent. Trails cannot be transplanted across contexts.

◆ Key Takeaway

Navigation labels are promises. If kept, the user advances. If broken, they leave — not because content is missing, but because the path was built for someone else.

Theoretical Framework

◆ Theory

Mental Models &
Navigation as Design Artefact

Norman, 1988 — The Design of Everyday Things

Mental Model

The user's internal representation of the system. Not necessarily accurate — but they navigate according to their model, not the designer's.

Design Model

The model embedded by designers — made visible in every category label, grouping, and hierarchy decision.

The Gap

When user model ≠ design model, navigation fails. The user cannot find what they need — not because it's absent, but because the path doesn't match their expectations.

The Navigation Menu Is a Claim About Users

What gets a top-level slot versus what is buried three levels deep — each decision encodes an assumption about how users think about the domain. Reading any menu critically means reading the assumptions of its designers, and the market they designed for.

◆ Key Takeaway

When a user's mental model diverges from the design model, the interface fails that user structurally — regardless of content quality. IA determines access.

What assumptions does its navigation make about you? Can you identify from the structure alone who it was not designed for?

→"Wellbeing" is a top-level category — same weight as Banking. Financial vulnerability is a primary use case.
→Direct links to: Money Worries, Bereavement, Domestic Abuse, Mental Health, Power of Attorney — welfare infrastructure in the nav itself.

Same number of top-level categories; radically different worlds beneath them. The architecture is calibrated to its market. Information scent is almost entirely non-transferable across them.

Section 3 — User Journeys

Thinking in Journeys

User Journey Mapping

Traces a specific user toward a goal — capturing the path taken, emotional state at each step, friction points, and where they give up or succeed.

Journeys are always constructed for a named, specific user with a defined goal — not a generic "typical user". The specificity is the point: it makes IA assumptions legible.

Key question: Where does the scent break?

Scent Analysis at Each Step

At every decision point in a journey, evaluate: does the current label predict the next step reliably? Does the user's own vocabulary appear in the navigation? Is the user confident they're on the right path?

Scent quality is rated across the journey: Strong (user advances confidently) · Weak (user hesitates) · Broken (user backtracks or abandons)

Key question: Is the user's language in the menu?

We will trace three journeys through both menus. At each step, evaluate the scent quality — and notice when the IA's embedded assumptions serve or fail the user. The personas were chosen to represent users the IA was designed for, and users it was not.

Reading the Architecture

What Does Each IA Assume?

The UK IA assumes a user who…

…may be financially vulnerable and needs support framed in human terms

…treats investment as a specialist, infrequent activity

…wants managed, curated products — not direct market access

…operates within a single jurisdiction with no cross-border needs

…uses one app occasionally, not five apps habitually

The HK IA assumes a user who…

…reads market intelligence as part of a normal daily routine

…treats equities, bonds, IPOs, and warrants as everyday consumer products

…wants platform efficiency — multiple apps, multiple entry points, no hand-holding

…manages assets in multiple currencies and jurisdictions routinely

…is motivated by commercial opportunity — not seeking institutional reassurance

Neither assumption is wrong — both are calibrated to their market. The question is: what happens when the user doesn't match the assumption? And whose responsibility is it to know?

Practical Method

◆ Method

Card Sorting: Grounding IA
in User Evidence

Open Card Sort

Participants receive unlabelled content cards. They create their own groups and name them. No predefined categories — pure user taxonomy.

Output: the user's spontaneous vocabulary — their actual mental model of the domain.

Closed Card Sort

Same content cards, but category labels are predefined. Participants place each item into the most appropriate category — testing IA legibility.

Output: evidence of whether the IA is legible — or opaque — to the target population.

Applied to Our Case

An open sort with UK users produces "Wellbeing" and "Help" as natural groups. The same sort with HK users produces investment sub-categories the UK menu doesn't contain.

These menus are the outputs of market-calibrated sorting over years of user research.

◆ Key Takeaway

Information scent is the test — does the navigation work? Card sorting is the method that helps you build scent that works for your users. Used together: the empirical core of user-centred IA practice.

App-as-IA

Section 4 — Synthesis

The IA Is the Argument

"Wellbeing" as top-level

…is a claim: users in difficulty are a primary use case, not an edge case.

"Insights" as top-level

…is a claim: users consume market intelligence regularly and need direct access to it.

"Offers" as top-level

…is a claim: users are motivated by commercial opportunity and actively seek incentives.

5 apps instead of 1

…is a claim: financial life is modular, specialised, and too varied for a single interface.

None of these are neutral decisions.

Every IA decision is a claim about users. Some claims are made consciously — through research, card sorting, deliberate design. Others are made by default: replicating legacy structures, designing for the designer's own mental model.

The two HSBC menus make these invisible forces visible.

◆ Key Takeaway

For information professionals: you are often the people who make these decisions, inherit these structures, and — with the right tools — can identify when they are failing the people they were built to serve.

Section 4 — Synthesis

Key Takeaways

01

Navigation labels are promises — broken scent means abandonment

Pirolli & Card

Scent must match the target user's vocabulary. Failure is structural, not content-based.

02

IA embeds assumptions — those assumptions need evidence

Norman

Every label is a hypothesis about user mental models. Test it with card sorting and user journeys before building.

03

Same brand; radically different IA for different markets

Mental Models

IA is not portable. It must be grown from the mental models of its actual users — not transplanted from another context.

04

User journeys expose the gap between intended and actual users

Journey Mapping

Trace a journey for someone the system was not designed for — structural assumptions become immediately visible.

05

Card sorting grounds IA in user evidence, not intuition

Card Sorting

Open sort first — find users' vocabulary. Closed sort second — test legibility. Never build from a blank wireframe without evidence.

06

IA critique is a core professional competency

You will work with, maintain, and sometimes design these systems. Reading embedded assumptions — and identifying whose needs they serve — is professional literacy.
