A Globally Fragmented Internet

There is NO single global social media sphere

Instead: Multiple parallel ecosystems with limited overlap

Western Sphere

Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, TikTok, YouTube

~2.5B users

Chinese Sphere

WeChat, Weibo, Douyin, Xiaohongshu, Bilibili

~1B users

Other Spheres

VK (Russia), Telegram, LINE (Japan/Taiwan), KakaoTalk (Korea)

~500M+ each

These ecosystems rarely overlap - different users, different content, different rules

Cultural Differences in Social Media Use

Privacy vs Openness

Western: More personal sharing, influencer culture. Asian: More careful self-presentation, group harmony valued

Individual vs Collective

Western: "This is ME". Chinese/Asian: More contextual identity, different faces for different audiences

Commerce Integration

Western: Social + commerce separate. Chinese: Fully integrated - social commerce, live shopping normal

Platform Loyalty

Western: Multi-platform users. Chinese: Super-app ecosystems (do everything in WeChat)
