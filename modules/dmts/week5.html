Reading Statistics Critically

Not all statistics are created equal. Ask questions:

  • ‚ùì

    Who collected this data?

    Government? Company? Industry group? Academic researchers? Bias matters.

  • ‚ùì

    When was it collected?

    Is it current or outdated? Technology changes fast.

  • ‚ùì

    How was it measured?

    Survey? Actual usage data? Sample size? Methodology matters.

  • ‚ùì

    What's the context?

    UK data vs global? One company vs whole industry? Be specific.

Beware: Companies often cherry-pick impressive statistics

Citing Statistical Data

Always cite where your statistics come from

‚úì Good example:

"According to the Office for National Statistics (2023), 96% of UK households had internet access, up from 57% in 2006."

Clear source, specific date, exact figure

‚úó Bad example:

"Most people use smartphones these days."

No source, vague, no data

‚úì Good example:

"Smartphone users increased from 1 billion in 2012 to 6.9 billion in 2024 (Statista, 2024), representing a 590% growth."

Source, dates, specific figures, shows change
