Red Flags in Tech Sources

üö© No methodology explained

"Studies show..." - which studies? Who was surveyed? How?

üö© Suspiciously perfect numbers

"This technology will grow exactly 15% per year" - too precise = prediction not fact

üö© Promotional language

"Revolutionary", "game-changing", "disruption" - marketing words

üö© No author/organization listed

Who wrote this? What's their expertise? Accountability matters

üö© Conflicting interests

"Study by Google shows Google products are best" - really?

Best Practice: Triangulate Sources

Use multiple source types together for complete picture

Example research strategy:

  • 1. Tech sources: Get current statistics (market size, user numbers)
  • 2. Academic sources: Understand social/cultural implications
  • 3. Government stats: Verify adoption rates, demographics
  • 4. Compare: Do they agree? Contradictions reveal bias

Your assignment requires:

2 book chapters + 2 journal papers + official online sources = naturally triangulates!
