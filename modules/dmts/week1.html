What is a "Digital Technology"?

Before we study how technologies transform society, we need to define our terms clearly

Digital = Information as Numbers

Digital technologies encode information (text, images, sound, video) as binary code (1s and 0s) that computers can process, store, and transmit

Examples of digital vs analog:

Digital:

‚Ä¢ Digital cameras (pixels)

‚Ä¢ MP3 files (sampled audio)

‚Ä¢ Netflix (streaming data)

Analog:

‚Ä¢ Film cameras (chemical)

‚Ä¢ Vinyl records (grooves)

‚Ä¢ Broadcast TV (waves)

From Prototype to Everyday Life

Technologies go through predictable stages:

1

Invention & Prototype

Lab experiments, early demonstrations, technical proof-of-concept

2

Early Adoption

Expensive, niche markets, enthusiasts and professionals

3

Mass Market

Affordable, widely available, becoming "normal"

4

Ubiquity & "Domestication"

Invisible infrastructure, taken for granted, deeply embedded in daily life

Your report will trace this journey with statistical evidence

Key Concept: Domestication

Domestication: The process by which a new technology becomes familiar, routine, and integrated into everyday practices and social relationships

Think about mobile phones:

1990s: "Only business people need mobile phones"

2000s: "Everyone should have a phone for emergencies"

2010s: "I can't imagine life without my smartphone"

2020s: Checking phone is first thing in morning, last thing at night

Domestication isn't just adoption - it's transformation of daily life and identity
