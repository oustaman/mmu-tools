Apply all five criteria to each source

Currency: Timeliness of Information

Questions to ask:

  • ‚Ä¢ When was this published?
  • ‚Ä¢ Has the information been updated or revised?
  • ‚Ä¢ Is this current enough for my topic?
  • ‚Ä¢ Are the links/references still working and current?
Fast-changing topics: Need sources from last 2-5 years
(AI, social media, technology)
Slower-changing topics: Older sources acceptable
(art history, philosophy, foundational theories)

Relevance: Fit with Your Needs

Questions to ask:

  • ‚Ä¢ Does this directly address my research topic?
  • ‚Ä¢ Is this at an appropriate level? (Not too basic, not too advanced)
  • ‚Ä¢ Who is the intended audience?
  • ‚Ä¢ Would I actually cite this in my work?

Red flag: If you're struggling to see how this source helps your argument, it's probably not relevant enough

Relevance is about YOUR specific needs

Authority: Credibility of Author & Publisher

Questions about the author:

  • ‚Ä¢ Who is the author? What are their credentials?
  • ‚Ä¢ Are they affiliated with a university or research institution?
  • ‚Ä¢ Have they published other work on this topic?
  • ‚Ä¢ Do other scholars cite their work?

Good sign: Author has PhD, works at university, has published multiple peer-reviewed articles

Authority: Publisher Reputation

Questions about the publisher:

  • ‚Ä¢ Who published this? Reputable journal or publisher?
  • ‚Ä¢ Is it peer-reviewed?
  • ‚Ä¢ Is the journal indexed in major databases?
  • ‚Ä¢ Does the publisher have editorial standards?
Trusted: Oxford University Press, Sage, Taylor & Francis, IEEE
Warning: Unknown publishers, pay-to-publish journals, no peer review process

Accuracy: Reliability of Information

Questions to ask:

  • ‚Ä¢ Does the source provide evidence for claims?
  • ‚Ä¢ Are there citations and references?
  • ‚Ä¢ Can you verify the information elsewhere?
  • ‚Ä¢ Are there obvious errors or typos?
  • ‚Ä¢ Does it use appropriate methodology?

Academic sources should always back up claims with evidence

Purpose: Why Was This Created?

Questions to ask:

  • ‚Ä¢ What is the purpose? Inform, persuade, sell, entertain?
  • ‚Ä¢ Does the author have a conflict of interest?
  • ‚Ä¢ Is there advertising or commercial influence?
  • ‚Ä¢ Is the language objective or emotionally charged?
  • ‚Ä¢ Are multiple perspectives presented?

Remember: All sources have some bias. The question is whether it's transparent and acknowledged.

Selecting Your Final 4 Sources

Assignment requirement: 4 sources of DIFFERENT types

Strategy for selection:

  • 1. Start with your strongest source - usually a recent peer-reviewed journal article
  • 2. Pick complementary types - one that provides context (book), one with data (report)
  • 3. Ensure all pass CRAAP test - don't include weak sources just to meet type requirement
  • 4. Check they work together - do they build a cohesive argument?

Documenting Rejected Sources

Assignment requirement: Explain why you rejected at least 1 source

Good reasons to reject a source:

  • ‚ùå Too old: Published 15 years ago, field has moved on
  • ‚ùå Not relevant enough: Only tangentially related to my topic
  • ‚ùå Weak authority: Author unknown, journal not peer-reviewed
  • ‚ùå Poor quality: No evidence, methodology unclear
  • ‚ùå Wrong type: Already have 2 journal articles, needed a book

Rejection shows critical thinking
