Section 3: Source Evaluation & Selection

Word count: 600 words

What to include:

  • 1. 4 different academic source types - identify and describe each
  • 2. Evaluation criteria applied - use CRAAP test for each source
  • 3. Why you chose them - explain selection rationale
  • 4. 1 rejected source example - and why it was rejected

Presenting Your 4 Sources

For each source, clearly identify:
  • ‚Ä¢ Type: Journal article, book chapter, report, or conference paper?
  • ‚Ä¢ Full citation: Author, year, title
  • ‚Ä¢ Brief summary: What does this source cover? (2-3 sentences)
  • ‚Ä¢ Relevance: How does it relate to your research topic?

Structure: introduce each source before evaluating it

Evaluating Each Source with CRAAP

Apply all 5 criteria to each source:

Currency: When published? Is it current enough for this topic?
Relevance: How directly does it address your research question?
Authority: Who's the author? Publisher credentials? Peer-reviewed?
Accuracy: Evidence provided? References included? Verifiable?
Purpose: Why was it written? Any bias? Objective analysis?

Don't just list criteria - analyze and explain

Example: Evaluating a Journal Article

Source 1: Journal Article
Smith, J. (2023) 'Artificial intelligence in graphic design practice', Design Studies, 45(2), pp. 123-145.

This peer-reviewed article examines how AI tools are being integrated into professional design workflows through case studies of five design agencies.

Evaluation: The article demonstrates strong currency (published 2023) which is essential given AI's rapid development. Relevance is high as it directly addresses my research focus on AI's impact on design practice. The author's authority is established through her position as Professor of Design at Stanford University and extensive publication record (20+ journal articles). Accuracy is evident through the detailed methodology, 45 references, and peer-review process. The purpose is clearly academic research with no apparent commercial bias, presenting both benefits and challenges of AI adoption.

  • ‚Ä¢ Identify the source clearly (full citation)

Providing In-Text Citation Examples

Show variety in your citation examples:
  • 1. Single author citation: (Smith, 2023) or Smith (2023) argues...
  • 2. Multiple authors: (Smith and Jones, 2022) or (Smith et al., 2023)
  • 3. Direct quote with page number: Smith (2023, p. 45) states...
  • 4. Multiple sources for one point: (Smith, 2023; Jones, 2022)

Include actual example sentences from your writing
