Types of Academic Databases

Multidisciplinary
Cover all subjects - good starting point
Examples: Academic Search Complete, JSTOR, ProQuest
Subject-Specific
Focus on one field - more specialized
Examples: PsycINFO (psychology), IEEE Xplore (engineering)
Free vs. Subscription
Google Scholar (free) vs. university databases (paid, but free for students)

Different databases for different needs

How to Access Library Databases

1
Go to your university library website
Usually library.youruni.ac.uk or similar
2
Log in with your university credentials
Same login as Moodle/email
3
Look for "Databases" or "Find Articles"
Browse by subject or search by name

Your library pays - you get free access

Getting Full-Text Articles

1
Look for PDF or Full-Text links
Usually clearly labeled in search results
2
Check "Find It @ Your Library" buttons

3

If not available, your library can get it (takes 3-7 days)

Never pay for articles - use library resources
