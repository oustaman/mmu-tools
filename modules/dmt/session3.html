Database Exploration

Finding Quality Academic Sources

Beyond Google: Why Databases Matter

Google finds:
• Everything on the public web
• Blogs, news, Wikipedia, random sites
• SEO-optimized content (not necessarily quality)
Academic Databases find:
• Peer-reviewed research
• Scholarly journals and books
• Quality-controlled content

For university work, databases give you credible sources

What Are Academic Databases?

Think of them as specialized libraries containing millions of academic resources:

  • 📚 Journal articles - research studies published in academic journals
  • 📖 Books and chapters - scholarly monographs and edited collections
  • 📊 Conference papers - presentations from academic conferences
  • 📄 Reports - research reports from institutions and organizations

Curated collections of credible research

Types of Academic Databases

Multidisciplinary
Cover all subjects - good starting point
Examples: Academic Search Complete, JSTOR, ProQuest
Subject-Specific
Focus on one field - more specialized
Examples: PsycINFO (psychology), IEEE Xplore (engineering)
Free vs. Subscription
Google Scholar (free) vs. university databases (paid, but free for students)

Different databases for different needs

How to Access Library Databases

1
Go to your university library website
Usually library.youruni.ac.uk or similar
2
Log in with your university credentials
Same login as Moodle/email
3
Look for "Databases" or "Find Articles"
Browse by subject or search by name

Your library pays - you get free access

How to Compare Databases

For your assignment, compare 3 databases using these criteria:

  • • Coverage: What subjects? What types of sources?
  • • Date range: How far back does it go?
  • • Full-text availability: Can you read articles immediately?
  • • Search features: What filters and tools available?
  • • Results quality: Are results relevant to your topic?

Test the same search in each database

Example: Google Scholar

Strengths:
✓ Free and easy to access
✓ Covers all subjects
✓ Shows citation counts
✓ Simple interface
Limitations:
✗ Not all sources are peer-reviewed
✗ Limited search filters
✗ No quality control
✗ Full-text often behind paywalls

Good starting point, but verify quality

Example: JSTOR

Strengths:
✓ High-quality, peer-reviewed journals
✓ Full-text PDFs available
✓ Covers humanities and social sciences
✓ Archive goes back decades
Limitations:
✗ Moving wall - newest articles delayed
✗ Limited STEM coverage
✗ Requires institutional access

Excellent for established research

Example: Academic Search Complete

Strengths:
✓ Multidisciplinary - covers everything
✓ Current articles (no moving wall)
✓ Advanced search features
✓ Full-text for most articles
Limitations:
✗ Can be overwhelming - too many results
✗ Interface takes getting used to
✗ Quality varies by journal

Great all-rounder for most topics

Getting Full-Text Articles

1
Look for PDF or Full-Text links
Usually clearly labeled in search results
2
Check "Find It @ Your Library" buttons
Links to other databases with full-text
3
Request through Inter-Library Loan
If not available, your library can get it (takes 3-7 days)

Never pay for articles - use library resources

Refining Results with Filters

Most databases let you narrow results after searching:

  • 📅 Date range: Last 5 years, last 10 years, specific years
  • 📑 Source type: Academic journals, books, conference papers
  • ✓ Peer-reviewed only: Ensures scholarly quality
  • 📄 Full-text only: Show only articles you can read immediately
  • 🌍 Language: English, or include other languages

Filters help manage thousands of results

Advanced Search Features

Search fields:
Search in title, abstract, full-text, or author name
Subject headings:
Databases tag articles with controlled vocabulary - very precise
Citation tracking:
Find articles that cite a key source (forward search)
Or see what a source cites (backward search)

These features save time and improve results

Saving and Exporting Results

💾 Create folders: Most databases let you save results to your account
📧 Email results: Send articles to yourself for later
📋 Export citations: Download references in various formats
🔗 Copy permalink: Get a stable URL to return to the article

Don't lose track of good sources

Troubleshooting Database Issues

Problem: Can't access database
→ Check you're logged in. Try accessing from campus network or use VPN
Problem: Can't find full-text
→ Look for "Find It" links or try another database. Ask library for help
Problem: Too many/too few results
→ Adjust Boolean operators and filters. Review Session 2 on search strategy
Problem: Results not relevant
→ Try different keywords. Check if you're searching the right subject area
