Section 1: Research Planning & Strategy

Word count: 400 words

What to include:

  • 1. Research topic selection - clearly defined topic
  • 2. Keyword development - table showing progression of searches
  • 3. Boolean operators - how you used AND, OR, NOT
  • 4. Database selection rationale - why you chose specific databases

Tell the story of how you planned your research

Writing About Topic Selection

What to write:
  • • State your topic clearly and specifically
  • • Explain why this topic interests you
  • • Mention if you narrowed or refined it from an initial idea
  • • Note the scope - what aspects you'll focus on
Example:
"My research topic focuses on the impact of artificial intelligence on graphic design workflows. I selected this topic because of recent developments in AI image generation tools and their implications for professional designers. Initially, I considered exploring AI in creative industries broadly, but narrowed it to graphic design specifically to allow for more focused research."

Keyword Development Table

Show how your keywords evolved through multiple searches:

Search # Keywords Used Results Refinement
1 AI design 50,000 Too broad
2 AI AND "graphic design" 8,500 More focused
3 (AI OR "artificial intelligence") AND "graphic design" AND workflow 342 Manageable

Show your iterative process

Explaining Your Boolean Strategy

What to explain:
  • • How you used AND to narrow results
  • • How you used OR to capture synonyms
  • • Whether you used NOT to exclude topics
  • • How you combined operators for complex searches
Example:
"I used the OR operator to capture different terms for artificial intelligence (AI OR 'machine learning' OR 'generative AI'), then combined this with AND to require 'graphic design' must appear. This ensured I captured relevant articles regardless of which AI terminology authors used."

Database Selection Rationale

Explain why you chose each database:
  • • Subject coverage - does it match your topic?
  • • Source types - journals, books, conference papers?
  • • Currency - how recent are the sources?
  • • Access - full-text availability?
Example:
"I selected Academic Search Complete for its multidisciplinary coverage, JSTOR for access to established design journals, and IEEE Xplore for technology-focused perspectives on AI. This combination ensured I accessed both design theory and technical implementation research."

Section 2: Database Navigation & Search Results

Word count: 500 words

What to include:

  • 1. Comparison of 3 academic databases - features, strengths, limitations
  • 2. Search refinement process - how you adjusted searches
  • 3. Results analysis - what you found, quality, relevance

Demonstrate your search expertise

Comparing Your 3 Databases

Create a comparison discussing:

For each database, discuss:
  • • Subject coverage and scope
  • • Types of sources available
  • • Date range of materials
  • • Search interface and features
  • • Full-text availability
  • • Quality/relevance of results for YOUR topic

Don't just describe - compare and evaluate

Database Comparison Example

"Academic Search Complete provided the broadest coverage with over 8,500 results for my initial search. However, many results were tangentially related to my topic. The database's advanced filters proved useful for narrowing by publication type and date range.

JSTOR offered fewer results (approximately 340) but with higher relevance, focusing on established design journals. The main limitation was the 'moving wall' which meant the most recent articles from the past year were unavailable.

IEEE Xplore provided the most technical perspective with 156 results, primarily from computer science and engineering conferences. While these sources offered valuable technical insights, they required more interpretation to relate to design practice."

Documenting Search Refinement

Show how you adjusted your approach:
  • • What filters did you apply? (date, peer-reviewed, full-text)
  • • Did you search in specific fields? (title, abstract, full-text)
  • • How did results change with each refinement?
  • • What worked well? What didn't?
Example:
"Initially, searching full-text returned too many irrelevant results. Restricting my search to titles and abstracts reduced results from 8,500 to 450, with significantly higher relevance. Adding a date filter (2018-2024) further narrowed to 287 results."

Analyzing Your Results

Discuss what you discovered:
  • • Quantity - how many results in each database?
  • • Quality - were results relevant and authoritative?
  • • Currency - how recent were the sources?
  • • Access - could you get full-text easily?
  • • Patterns - any themes or gaps in the literature?

Be specific with numbers and observations

Writing Tips for Sections 1-2

✓ Use first person
"I selected...", "I refined...", "My strategy was..."
✓ Be specific and detailed
Include actual numbers, database names, search terms
✓ Show your thinking
Explain why you made decisions, not just what you did
✓ Include evidence
Screenshots in appendix, tables in main text
✗ Don't just list
Write in paragraphs, not bullet points

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Too vague
"I searched for information" → Be specific about what and where
❌ Just describing databases
Don't copy from database "About" pages - discuss YOUR experience
❌ No evidence of refinement
Show multiple search attempts, not just one perfect search
❌ Ignoring word count
Section 1 = 400 words, Section 2 = 500 words (±10%)
