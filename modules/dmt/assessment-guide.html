üìñ What is this assessment?

You're going to choose a research topic that interests you, then document how you find, evaluate, and cite academic sources about it. Think of this as a "research diary" where you show your process step-by-step.

You'll write about your experiences using academic databases, explain why you chose certain sources over others, and demonstrate that you can reference them properly. At the end, you'll reflect on what you learned.

Bottom line: This isn't about becoming an expert on your topic‚Äîit's about becoming skilled at academic research itself.

Section 3: Source Evaluation (600 words)

Using CRAAP test to select your best 4 sources ‚Ä¢ Click to see examples

‚ñº

What to include:

  • CRAAP evaluation: Apply Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose to each source
  • Your final 4 sources: Which ones you selected and detailed justification
  • Sources you rejected: Why certain sources didn't make the cut
  • Source variety: Explain how your 4 sources offer different perspectives or types
CRAAP Reminder: Currency (when?), Relevance (does it fit?), Authority (who wrote it?), Accuracy (is it reliable?), Purpose (why was it written?)

‚úì Strong Example

"Using the CRAAP test, I evaluated my 8 potential sources and selected the strongest 4. Source 1 (Chen et al., 2023) scored high on all criteria: published recently (Currency), directly addresses AI in music production (Relevance), authored by researchers at MIT Media Lab (Authority), peer-reviewed with extensive references (Accuracy), and aims to inform academic and industry audiences (Purpose). Section 4: Citation Management (450 words)

Harvard referencing and bibliography ‚Ä¢ Click to see examples

‚ñº

What to include:

  • Your 4 sources in Harvard style: Complete reference list entry for each
  • In-text citation examples: Show different scenarios (paraphrase, direct quote, multiple authors)
  • Citation tools discussion: What helped you manage references (Zotero, Mendeley, ZBib, etc.)
  • Challenges and solutions: What was tricky about referencing and how you figured it out
Harvard referencing required (not separately marked). Use MMU Cite Them Right Harvard. Check for: author-date format, alphabetical order, hanging indent, proper capitalization.
üîß Helpful tool: Try ZBib.org - a free citation generator that helps create Harvard references quickly. Always double-check against Cite Them Right!
