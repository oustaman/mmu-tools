Keywords & Search Planning

From Topic to Search Strategy

The Problem with Natural Language

You think:

"I want to learn about how Instagram affects young people's mental health"

Database thinks:

"Instagram" OR "affects" OR "young people's" OR "mental health"?

  • • Too broad → thousands of irrelevant results
  • • Too narrow → zero results

Databases don't understand sentences - they match WORDS

The Keyword Strategy

  • 1. Break your topic into CONCEPTS
  • 2. Find SYNONYMS for each concept
  • 3. Combine them systematically

Example:

"Social media" = Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, Twitter, Snapchat

Think like a database, not like a human

Example Breakdown

Topic: "AI impact on graphic design"

Concept 1: AI
→ artificial intelligence, machine learning, automation, generative AI
Concept 2: Graphic design
→ visual design, visual communication, branding, typography
Concept 3: Impact
→ effect, influence, change, transformation

One topic = multiple keyword combinations

Boolean Operators: Your Search Superpowers

AND

Both terms must appear

OR

Either term can appear

NOT

Exclude terms

These operators control your search precision

Boolean in Action

AI AND graphic design
Results must contain BOTH terms
Instagram OR TikTok
Results can contain EITHER term
design NOT fashion
Exclude fashion design results
(Instagram OR TikTok) AND mental health
Combine operators with parentheses

Operators let you be specific

Quotation Marks: Exact Phrases

social media
Finds: social anywhere + media anywhere (millions of results)
"social media"
Finds: exact phrase only (much more precise)
"graphic design" AND "artificial intelligence"
Combine exact phrases with Boolean operators

Use quotes when word order matters

Wildcards & Truncation

Asterisk (*) - Multiple characters
design*
Finds: design, designer, designing, designs, designate
Question mark (?) - Single character
organi?ation
Finds: organisation (UK) AND organization (US)

Wildcards catch variations automatically

Advanced Search Operators

Search in title only:
intitle:"user experience"
More relevant results - title indicates main topic
Search specific website:
site:bbc.co.uk climate change
Only search within BBC website
Search by file type:
filetype:pdf design research
Find only PDFs (great for academic papers)

Target exactly what you need

Example: Black Friday Deals

September-October
"Black Friday predictions 2024", "best Black Friday deals"
Peak search volume begins

"Black Friday sales live", "Black Friday laptop deals", "where to shop Black Friday"
Highest search volume

Search trends follow real-world patterns

When You Get Zero Results

  • 1. Check spelling - databases are literal
  • 2. Remove some ANDs - you might be too narrow
  • 3. Try broader terms - "car" instead of "Honda Civic"
  • 4. Remove quotation marks - exact phrases might not exist
  • 5. Try synonyms - "smartphone" vs "mobile phone"

Zero results = search is too specific

When You Get Too Many Results

  • 1. Add more ANDs - narrow with additional concepts
  • 2. Use quotation marks - "social media" instead of social media
  • 3. Add NOT - exclude irrelevant topics
  • 4. Use intitle: - search only in titles
  • 5. Add date limits - last 5 years only

Too many results = search is too broad

What Makes a Source Credible?

✓ Peer-Reviewed Journals
Experts reviewed before publication - highest credibility
✓ Academic Books & Reports
Published by universities or research institutions
⚠ News & Media
Check reputation - BBC vs random blog
✗ Social Media & Blogs
Anyone can post - verify claims elsewhere

Always ask: Who wrote this? Why? For whom?

Search Strategy: Start Broad, Then Narrow

1
First search: Use broad terms, see what exists
2
Second search: Add more specific terms
3
Third search: Combine concepts with AND
✓
Keep track of what works!

Searching is iterative, not one-and-done
