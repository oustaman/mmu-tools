Problem Analysis: What to Include

200-250 words covering:

  • 1.What problems existed: Specific issues users faced
  • 2.Evidence: Data, quotes, observations proving these problems
  • 3.Why it mattered: Impact on users (time, frustration, task failure)
  • 4.Context: When/where these problems occurred

This is either "enhancing existing experience" OR "creating new experience"

Example: Problem Analysis

Library Study Room Booking (234 words)

Students at MMU struggled to find and book available study rooms efficiently. The current booking system required users to navigate through multiple menus‚Äîclicking from the library homepage to services, then facilities, then booking‚Äîtaking an average of 7 clicks and 3 minutes to reach the availability calendar. User reviews on app stores showed consistent complaints: "By the time I found a room, it was already taken" and "Too slow, just gave up."

Analytics data revealed a 73% abandonment rate on the booking flow, with most users leaving after the second screen. Observational research showed students checking multiple times throughout the day, wasting 10-15 minutes between lectures trying to secure study space. During exam periods, this problem intensified, with booking times extending to 5+ minutes due to high demand and limited availability information.

This inefficiency directly impacted students' academic performance and stress levels. Lost study time meant less preparation for assessments, while the frustration of the booking process discouraged library use entirely. Students reported choosing less optimal study environments (caf√©s, home) simply to avoid the booking system, undermining the library's role as a primary learning space.

Example: Solution Evaluation

Library Study Room Booking Solution (221 words)

My design addresses the core availability problem by displaying all free study rooms directly on the home screen, using color-coding (green=available now, yellow=available soon, grey=booked). This eliminates the 7-click navigation process entirely. Users can see room status, capacity, and equipment at a glance, applying filters without leaving the main view.

The booking flow is reduced to two steps: select room and confirm time. Real-time updates prevent the "already taken" frustration by showing live availability as other students book. Error prevention is built in through availability constraints‚Äîusers cannot select unavailable slots, and the system confirms bookings immediately.

Expected improvements include reducing booking time from 3 minutes to approximately 30 seconds, cutting abandonment rates from 73% to under 30%, and eliminating navigation confusion entirely. Success would be measured through: completion rates, time-on-task metrics, user satisfaction surveys, and reduced support requests about the booking system. The design applies visibility of system status (Heuristic #1) and error prevention (Heuristic #5), directly addressing the usability issues identified in research.

Recommendations: What to Include

150-200 words covering:

  • 1.Next steps: What should be developed next?
  • 2.Additional testing: What needs validation?
  • 3.Future features: What could be added later?
  • 4.Implementation: Realistic considerations
  • 5.Scalability: How could this grow?

What Makes Good Recommendations?

Specific

Not "improve it" but "add push notifications for booking confirmations"

Actionable

Something that can actually be done with resources available

Realistic

Acknowledges constraints (time, budget, technical limitations)

Prioritized

What's most important to do first?
