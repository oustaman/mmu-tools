How to use the
Idea Generator

// a quick guide — takes 2 minutes to read

1

Pick options across four dimensions

The matrix has four rows: Target User, Core Problem, Platform, and Key Mechanic. Click one chip in each row. You don't have to fill all four to get started — even two or three is enough to see where an idea might go.

2

Or just hit ⚡ Generate

Don't overthink it. Hit Generate and the tool picks a random combination for you. Keep hitting it — new combinations appear instantly. You're looking for the one that makes you think "…wait, that could actually work."

3

Lock what you like, randomise the rest

See a row you want to keep? Click the 🔓 lock icon to freeze it. Now hit Generate again — everything else changes, your locked row stays. This is how you explore variations without starting from scratch every time.

4

Get an AI concept brief

Found a combination you like? Hit ✦ Brief. An AI writes a short product concept summary — just enough to help you decide if the idea is worth developing further.

Remember: the brief is a spark, not a spec. Your job is to take the seed and ask the real questions: who is this actually for? What do they struggle with? Would they use this every day? That thinking is the UX work.
5

Add your own options

The defaults are starting points. Each row (except Platform) has a + add own… field. Type in something more specific — a niche user group, a problem you've personally observed — and press Enter to add it to that row.
