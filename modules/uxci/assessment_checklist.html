Target Users: MMU undergraduate and postgraduate students, primarily aged 18-30, who require quiet study spaces for individual work or small group collaboration.

üìÑ Slide 6: UCD Principles (200-250 words)

EXAMPLE CONTENT (you can choose ANY 2 UCD principles):

Principle 1: Visibility of System Status (example - choose your own!)

Users always know what's happening. The home screen shows real-time room availability with color coding (green=available, red=booked). When booking, a progress indicator shows the action is processing. After booking, immediate confirmation appears with room details. This principle addresses the previous system's lack of feedback, which left users uncertain if bookings succeeded.

Principle 2: User Control & Freedom (example - choose your own!)

Users can cancel bookings up to 5 minutes before the reserved time with one tap. An "undo" button appears after accidental bookings. Easy exit from any screen via persistent navigation. This empowers users and reduces booking anxiety, as they're not locked into mistakes. Previous system had no cancellation option within the app...
